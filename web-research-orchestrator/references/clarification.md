# Clarification Preflight

Use this when the user's topic is broad, underspecified, or likely to branch into multiple major research directions.

## When to stop before full research

Pause and clarify when:

- The topic is a generic noun or field: "research chocolate", "research AI", "research batteries".
- The purpose is unclear: learning, buying, investing, product planning, article writing, technical choice, policy analysis.
- Important dimensions are missing: timeframe, geography, language, audience, output depth, source type.
- The result could reasonably be about history, market, technology, people, health, regulation, culture, or methods.

## Quick scan rules

- Use low reasoning and a small number of searches.
- Do not answer the research question yet.
- Extract only likely branches, vocabulary, and scope choices.
- Keep the user-facing question short.

## Question pattern

Use a short multiple-choice question with 5-7 options and an "other" option. End by saying you will proceed once the scope is selected.

Example:

```text
"Research chocolate" is broad, so I need to narrow the target before full research. Which angle should I prioritize?

1. History: cacao use, European spread, modern chocolate industry
2. Production: fermentation, roasting, conching, bean-to-bar workflow
3. Ingredients: cacao, sugar, milk solids, fats, additives, quality differences
4. Market: brands, price bands, consumption trends, major companies
5. People: chocolatiers, bean-to-bar makers, awards
6. Health and nutrition: cocoa polyphenols, sugar, risks, evidence quality
7. Other: specify your angle
```
